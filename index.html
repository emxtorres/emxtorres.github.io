About me:

I'm Emerson Torres, a Salvadoran mathematician who graduated with honors from the University of El Salvador. I'm passionate about teaching, research, and applying mathematics to science and technology. I've worked as a Math and Science teacher at different levels — from high-performance programs like Jóvenes Talento and the ASD-CIDE program to private schools. Altogether, I've accumulated over 1300 hours of teaching experience, designing materials, assessing learning, and using digital platforms like Moodle, Teams, and Google Classroom to enhance education. I've also participated in several international advanced training schools, including CIMPA Schools (in Topology and Geometry), EMALCA (El Salvador and Costa Rica), and workshops focused on mathematical modeling and data science with Python, strengthening my academic and research profile. Languages are another personal goal. Spanish is my native language, I have an intermediate level of English (TOEFL iBT: 71), and I'm currently learning Portuguese.

My academic interests include:

  • Algorithms
  • Computational Complexity Theory
  • Theory of Computation
  • Linear Optimization
  • Quantum Computing
  • Graph Theory

Beyond mathematics, I enjoy video editing, graphic design, music production, and programming (mainly in Python and C). My goal is to keep growing professionally in research, teaching, and developing tech-based mathematical solutions.
